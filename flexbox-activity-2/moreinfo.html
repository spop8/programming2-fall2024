Cape Leadwort

  • Native: South Africa
  • Plant Family: Plumbago Auriculata
  • Number of Species: 1000+
  • Uses: Relieves toothaches and soothes sunburns
  • Fun Fact: Can be grown indoor or outdoor

Dahlia

  • Native: Mexico
  • Plant Family: Asteraceae
  • Number of Species: 42
  • Uses: Aztecs used them as food and also to treat epilepsy
  • Fun Fact: Marie Antionette’s favourite flower

Geranium

  • Native: North America
  • Plant Family: Geraniaceaeg
  • Number of Species: 422
  • Uses: Made into essential oils used for relaxation
  • Fun Fact: Repels wasps and mosquitoes

Dimorphotheca Ecklonis

  • Native: South Africa
  • Plant Family: Asteraceae
  • Number of Species: 19
  • Uses: Used as ornamental plant
  • Fun Fact: Named after a Danish botanical collector

Lantana

  • Native: South America
  • Plant Family: Verbenas
  • Number of Species: 150
  • Uses: Can help treat fevers, coughs, colds, and more
  • Fun Fact: Can change color as they mature

Chocolate Cosmos

  • Native: Mexico
  • Plant Family: Daisy
  • Number of Species: 25
  • Uses: Vital for pollination for bees and butterflies
  • Fun Fact: They actually do smell like chocolate

Echinacea

  • Native: United States
  • Plant Family: Daisy
  • Number of Species: 10
  • Uses: Used to treat infections and wounds
  • Fun Fact: The name comes from latin meaning "sea urchin"

Peony

  • Native: Europe and Asia
  • Plant Family: Paeoniaceae
  • Number of Species: 30+
  • Uses: Sometimes used to treat psoriasis
  • Fun Fact: Peonies thrive in cold weather
